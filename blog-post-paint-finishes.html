The color you choose gets all the attention, but your paint's sheen—its level of glossiness—plays an equally important role in how your rooms look and perform. The right finish enhances your walls' beauty while standing up to the demands of daily life. Here's everything you need to know about choosing the perfect sheen for every room in your mountain home.

Understanding Paint Sheen

Paint sheen refers to how much light the dried paint reflects. Sheens range from completely flat (no reflection) to high-gloss (mirror-like reflection). The amount of sheen affects three important factors:

  • Appearance: Higher sheens make colors appear more vibrant and can make rooms feel brighter. Lower sheens create a softer, more sophisticated look.
  • Durability: Generally, higher-sheen paints are more durable and easier to clean. The glossier the finish, the harder the surface.
  • Imperfection visibility: Flat paints hide wall imperfections while glossy paints highlight every bump, dent, and repair.

There's no single "best" sheen—the right choice depends on the room's function, the wall's condition, and your aesthetic preferences. Most homes use multiple sheens throughout different spaces.

Flat & Matte Finishes

0-10% Sheen

The Sophisticate's Choice

Flat and matte finishes absorb light rather than reflecting it, creating a velvety, elegant appearance. They're the preferred choice for high-end interiors because they provide the truest color representation and create a refined, gallery-like atmosphere.

Best For: Ceilings, formal living rooms, dining rooms, master bedrooms, and any room where you want a sophisticated, non-reflective look. Excellent for hiding wall imperfections.

Considerations: Traditional flat paints were difficult to clean, but modern premium matte paints (like Sherwin-Williams Emerald or Benjamin Moore Regal Select) offer excellent washability. They're still less durable than higher sheens, so they're not ideal for high-traffic areas or homes with young children.

Eggshell Finish

10-25% Sheen

The Versatile Standard

Named for its resemblance to an eggshell's subtle luster, this finish offers the best of both worlds: a soft, elegant appearance with improved durability and cleanability compared to flat paint.

Best For: Living rooms, bedrooms, hallways, and dining rooms. The go-to choice for most wall surfaces in residential settings.

Considerations: Eggshell is our most-recommended finish for Vail Valley homes. It handles the occasional scuff or fingerprint well while maintaining an upscale appearance that complements mountain home aesthetics.

Satin Finish

25-35% Sheen

The Workhorse

Satin finish has a pearl-like sheen that's noticeably more lustrous than eggshell. It's highly durable, easy to clean, and stands up well to moisture—making it ideal for busy households and high-use areas.

Best For: Kitchens, bathrooms, mudrooms, kids' rooms, hallways, and any high-traffic area. Also excellent for woodwork and trim.

Considerations: The increased sheen makes application technique more important—roller marks and brush strokes are more visible. Professional application ensures a smooth, consistent finish.

Semi-Gloss Finish

35-70% Sheen

The Trim Specialist

Semi-gloss offers a radiant sheen that's highly reflective without being mirror-like. It's extremely durable, moisture-resistant, and easy to clean—even stubborn stains wipe away easily.

Best For: Trim, doors, window frames, cabinets, and bathroom/kitchen walls. Creates beautiful contrast when paired with lower-sheen wall paint.

Considerations: The high reflectivity shows every wall imperfection, so surfaces must be properly prepared. It's rarely used on walls except in bathrooms or kitchens where moisture resistance is paramount.

High-Gloss Finish

70%+ Sheen

The Statement Maker

High-gloss paint creates a dramatic, lacquer-like finish that's incredibly reflective and durable. It's the most stain-resistant and easiest to clean of all finishes.

Best For: Accent furniture, built-in cabinetry, front doors, and architectural details where you want to make a bold statement.

Considerations: High-gloss requires flawless surface preparation—any imperfection will be magnified. Professional spray application typically yields the best results. Use sparingly for maximum impact.

Room-by-Room Guide

Here's our recommended sheen for each area of your home:

Living Room

Walls: Eggshell or Matte
Trim: Semi-Gloss

Master Bedroom

Walls: Matte or Eggshell
Trim: Satin or Semi-Gloss

Kitchen

Walls: Satin or Semi-Gloss
Cabinets: Semi-Gloss

Bathroom

Walls: Satin or Semi-Gloss
Trim: Semi-Gloss

Kids' Rooms

Walls: Satin or Eggshell
Trim: Semi-Gloss

Hallways

Walls: Eggshell or Satin
Trim: Semi-Gloss

Ceilings

All Rooms: Flat
(hides imperfections, reduces glare)

Mudroom/Entry

Walls: Satin
Trim: Semi-Gloss

Special Considerations for Mountain Homes

Mountain homes present unique challenges that influence finish selection:

  • Dramatic natural light: Vail's intense sunlight streaming through large windows can create glare on high-sheen surfaces. Consider lower sheens in rooms with significant glazing.
  • Ski gear and outdoor equipment: Mudrooms and entryways see heavy use. Satin or semi-gloss finishes stand up better to wet boots, ski equipment, and frequent cleaning.
  • Rustic textures: If your walls have texture from wood paneling, exposed timber, or textured drywall, matte finishes complement these elements better than glossy ones.
  • Dry climate: Colorado's low humidity means walls stay cleaner longer, so you may be able to use lower-sheen finishes even in areas where satin might typically be recommended.

When in doubt, we recommend eggshell for walls and semi-gloss for trim throughout most of your home. This classic combination offers the perfect balance of beauty and practicality.
